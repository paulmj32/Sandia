SETH's MODEL: SGHOPM (publicly available one)
- want all variables to be available/easily forecasted in advance of an approaching hurricane
- uses RF for prediction and then Bayesian multiscale model to post-process/smooth spatial predictions (see, Reilly and Guikema 2015)

Final model (2014) ~ population density + wind gust + duration strong winds

DV: fraction of population w/o power at census tract spatial scale 
IVs:
(Population) 
- population density (started using population density in 2018 instead of total population or number of customers) 

(Hurricane Variables)
- parametric wind field model: max 3 second wind gust
- parametric wind field model: duration of time with wind exceeding 20m/s (design speed of poles) for each space

(Land Cover)
- 8 NLCD classes: water, developed, barren, forest, scrub, grassland, planted/cultivated, and wetland (fraction of each spatial unit)

(Soil and Precipitation ... see Han et al 2009a 2009b)
- soil moisture (Variable Infiltration Capacity model ... or STATSGO?) 
- long-term drought (SPI)
- mean annual precipitation (NOAA, proxy for vegetation) 

(Topographic ... see Han et al 2009a 2009b)
- elevation: mean, min, max, and sd
- aspect ratio
- topographic index
